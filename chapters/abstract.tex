\begin{abstract}

碳纳米管阵列因优良的电学、热学和力学性能，在储能、热管理和高性能复合材料等领域具有重要应用潜力。围绕其制备与应用研究，工艺条件如何影响形貌演化并进一步作用于结构状态，是碳纳米管阵列研究中的重要问题。现有研究主要面临两方面困难。一方面，工艺条件、形貌特征和生长机理等信息长期分散于文献、实验记录和图像分析结果中，缺少统一的结构化组织与可追溯证据支撑，难以为后续工艺变量筛选、机理分析和结果追溯提供可靠依据。另一方面，扫描电子显微镜（SEM）图像是表征碳纳米管阵列整体形貌的主要依据，但其中复杂细长交叠结构使样品级形貌特征难以稳定量化，后续分析因而缺少可靠的数据支撑。受此影响，当前相关研究仍缺少能够同时支撑形貌量化、证据组织和结果追溯的分析方法。

本文首先提出面向碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌关系分析的知识组织与检索增强方法。针对相关知识长期分散、难以直接调用的问题，该方法将文献、实验记录和图像分析结果中的工艺、形貌、机理和性能信息组织为可关联的结构化知识条目，进一步构建关系图，并结合关系约束检索算法（TCCER）组织检索结果与来源证据，为后续工艺变量筛选、机理分析和结果追溯提供知识支撑。

随后建立面向碳纳米管阵列 SEM 图像的样品级自动化形貌表征方法。针对复杂细长交叠结构前景难以稳定提取的问题，该方法采用 CNTSegNet-clDice 进行前景分割，并结合骨架分析与几何计算建立样品级量化流程，提取覆盖密度、取向度、表观直径、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度等形貌指标，为后续分析提供数据支撑。

在此基础上，本文提出知识增强支持的工艺—形貌关系分析方法。在知识证据支持下完成工艺变量收敛和背景窗口限定后，围绕当前 Fe 磁控溅射催化层和管式炉化学气相沉积（CVD）生长构成的样品体系，对 Fe 溅射功率、Fe 催化层厚度和退火时间开展统计比较与关系建模。结果表明，三类变量与取向度、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度之间存在稳定的趋势关系。其中，退火时间与取向下降和弯曲增强之间呈现最稳定的趋势关系，Fe 溅射功率主要改变不同工艺区间内的响应强弱及其出现区间，Fe 催化层厚度的影响较弱但持续存在。综合统计结果与知识证据，三类变量对阵列取向和弯曲响应的影响可在催化层形成、颗粒演化和群体耦合框架下理解。上述方法进一步在科研辅助分析平台中完成集成验证，表明样品记录、知识检索、自动化形貌表征和关系分析能够连续衔接。

\end{abstract}

\keywords{碳纳米管阵列；自动化表征；知识增强；工艺—形貌关系}

Carbon nanotube arrays have broad application prospects in energy storage, thermal management, and high-performance composites because of their excellent electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties. A central issue in their preparation and application is how process conditions drive morphology evolution and further affect structural state and performance. Existing studies mainly face two difficulties. First, process-morphology relationships in carbon nanotube arrays are strongly condition-dependent and mechanistically complex. Information on process conditions, morphology features, and growth mechanisms remains scattered across literature, experimental records, and image analysis results, lacking unified structured organization and traceable evidential support. As a result, it is difficult to provide an effective knowledge basis for interpreting the effects of key variables and tracing analytical results. Second, scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images are the primary basis for characterizing overall array morphology, yet the slender and overlapping structures in these images make stable quantification of sample-level morphology features difficult, leaving subsequent analysis without reliable data support. Consequently, current related studies still lack analytical methods that can jointly support morphology quantification, evidence organization, and result traceability.

First, a knowledge organization and relation-constrained retrieval method is proposed for process-morphology relationship analysis of carbon nanotube arrays. To address the long-term fragmentation and limited direct usability of relevant knowledge, the method organizes process, morphology, mechanism, and performance information from literature, experimental records, and image analysis results into linkable structured knowledge entries. It further constructs a relation graph and combines the relation-constrained retrieval algorithm (TCCER) with evidence organization, thereby providing knowledge support for process-variable screening, mechanism analysis, and result traceability.

Second, this study establishes a sample-level automated morphology characterization method for carbon nanotube array SEM images. To address unstable foreground extraction for complex slender and overlapping structures, the method uses CNTSegNet-clDice for foreground segmentation and combines skeleton analysis with geometric computation to establish a sample-level quantification pipeline. The pipeline extracts areal density, alignment, apparent diameter, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity as morphology indicators, providing data support for subsequent analysis.

On this basis, a knowledge-enhanced method for process-morphology relationship analysis is proposed. With knowledge support for process-variable convergence and background-window control, statistical comparison and relationship modeling are carried out for Fe sputtering power, Fe catalyst layer thickness, and annealing time in the current sample system based on Fe magnetron-sputtered catalyst layers and tube-furnace chemical vapor deposition (CVD) growth. The results show stable trend correspondences between these variables and alignment, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity. Annealing time most stably corresponds to reduced alignment and enhanced bending, Fe sputtering power mainly changes the response strength and the process intervals in which the response emerges, and the influence of Fe catalyst layer thickness is weaker but persistent. Taken together, statistical results and knowledge evidence place these effects within a framework of catalyst-layer formation, particle evolution, and collective coupling, supporting quantitative analysis, inductive interpretation, and result traceability for sample-level relations between process variables and morphology indicators. These methods are further integrated and validated in a research support platform, showing that sample records, knowledge retrieval, automated morphology characterization, and relationship analysis can be continuously linked.

\end{enabstract}
