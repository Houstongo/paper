\begin{abstract}

碳纳米管阵列因优良的电学、热学和力学性能，在储能、热管理和高性能复合材料等领域具有重要应用潜力。围绕阵列制备与应用开发，工艺条件如何影响形貌演化并进一步作用于结构状态和性能表现，是实现碳纳米管阵列可控制备与性能优化的关键问题。现有研究主要面临两方面困难。一方面，碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌关系具有较强的条件依赖性和机理复杂性，工艺条件、形貌特征和生长机理等信息长期分散于文献、实验记录和图像分析结果之中，缺少统一的结构化组织与可追溯证据支撑，因而难以为关键变量作用解释与结果追溯提供有效的知识基础。另一方面，扫描电子显微镜图像是分析阵列整体形貌的主要依据，但碳纳米管阵列在图像中表现为细长交叠结构，样品级形貌特征难以稳定量化，工艺—形貌分析因而缺少可靠的数据支撑。受此影响，当前碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌关系研究仍缺少能够同时支撑形貌量化、证据组织与结果追溯的分析方法。

本文首先建立面向碳纳米管阵列研究的知识组织与检索增强方法。针对相关知识长期分散、难以直接调用的问题，本文将工艺、形貌、机理和性能信息组织为可关联的结构化知识条目，进一步构建关系图，并通过关系约束检索算法 TCCER 为工艺变量筛选和机理分析提供知识基础。

随后建立以 CNTSegNet-clDice 为核心的样品级自动化形貌表征方法。针对碳纳米管阵列 SEM 图像中复杂细长结构前景难以稳定提取的问题，本文构建兼顾连通性保持的分割方法，并结合骨架分析与几何计算建立样品级量化流程，提取覆盖密度、取向度、表观直径、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度等形貌指标，为后续工艺—形貌分析提供数据基础。

在此基础上，本文结合知识增强检索结果与样品级形貌指标，对当前 Fe 磁控溅射催化层和热 CVD 生长构成的样品体系开展工艺—形貌关系分析，并围绕 Fe 溅射功率、Fe 催化层厚度和退火时间进行统计分析和随机森林建模。结果表明，三类变量与取向度、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度之间存在可建模的对应关系。其中，退火时间与取向下降及弯曲相关特征增强之间呈现最稳定的趋势关系，Fe 溅射功率主要改变不同工艺区间中的响应强弱及其出现区间，Fe 催化层厚度的贡献较弱但持续存在。结合知识证据可知，三类变量虽作用位置不同，但均通过催化层形成与颗粒演化影响阵列取向和弯曲响应。集成验证结果表明，本文建立的分析流程能够支持碳纳米管阵列样品级工艺—形貌关系的定量分析与机理解释。

\end{abstract}

\begin{enabstract}

Carbon nanotube arrays have broad application prospects in energy storage, thermal management, and high-performance composites because of their excellent electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties. A central issue in their preparation and application is how process conditions drive morphology evolution and further affect structural state and performance. Existing studies mainly face two difficulties. First, process-morphology relationships in carbon nanotube arrays are strongly condition-dependent and mechanistically complex. Information on process conditions, morphology features, and growth mechanisms remains scattered across literature, experimental records, and image analysis results, lacking unified structured organization and traceable evidential support. As a result, it is difficult to provide an effective knowledge basis for interpreting the effects of key variables and tracing analytical results. Second, scanning electron microscopy images are the primary basis for characterizing overall array morphology, yet the slender and overlapping structures in these images make stable quantification of sample-level morphology features difficult, leaving process-morphology analysis without reliable data support. Consequently, current studies on process-morphology relationships in carbon nanotube arrays still lack analytical methods that can jointly support morphology quantification, evidence organization, and result traceability.

First, a knowledge organization and retrieval enhancement method is developed for carbon nanotube array research. To address the long-term fragmentation and limited direct usability of relevant knowledge, process, morphology, mechanism, and performance information is organized into linkable structured knowledge entries and further assembled into a relation graph. The relation-constrained retrieval algorithm TCCER is then used to provide a knowledge basis for process-variable screening and mechanism analysis.

Second, a sample-level automated morphology characterization method centered on CNTSegNet-clDice is developed. To address the difficulty of stable foreground extraction for complex slender structures in carbon nanotube array scanning electron microscopy images, a connectivity-preserving segmentation method is combined with skeleton analysis and geometric computation to establish a sample-level quantification pipeline. The pipeline extracts areal density, alignment, apparent diameter, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity as morphology indicators, providing data support for subsequent process-morphology analysis.

On this basis, the thesis combines knowledge-enhanced retrieval results with sample-level morphology indicators to analyze process-morphology relationships in the current sample system based on Fe magnetron-sputtered catalyst layers and thermal CVD growth. Statistical analysis and random forest modeling are conducted for Fe sputtering power, Fe catalyst layer thickness, and annealing time. The results show modelable correspondences between these variables and alignment, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity. Annealing time exhibits the most stable trend relationship with reduced alignment and enhanced bending-related features. Fe sputtering power mainly changes the response strength and the interval in which the response appears across different process windows, whereas Fe catalyst layer thickness has a weaker but persistent contribution. Combined with the knowledge evidence, these variables, although acting at different stages, all influence array alignment and bending response through catalyst-layer formation and particle evolution. Integrated validation shows that the proposed workflow supports quantitative sample-level process-morphology analysis and mechanistic interpretation for carbon nanotube arrays.

\end{enabstract}
