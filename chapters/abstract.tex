\begin{abstract}

碳纳米管阵列因优良的电学、热学和力学性能，在储能器件、热界面材料、场发射器件及高性能复合材料等领域具有广阔应用前景。围绕阵列制备与应用开发，工艺条件如何作用于形貌演化并进一步影响结构状态与性能表现，是相关研究中的关键问题。现有研究面临两方面困难。其一，在形貌表征方面，扫描电子显微镜（SEM）图像是分析阵列整体形貌的主要依据，但碳纳米管阵列在图像中呈现出的细长交叠结构，使样品级形貌特征的稳定量化较为困难，工艺—形貌分析因而缺少可靠的数据支撑。其二，在关系分析方面，碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌关系具有较强的条件依赖性和机理复杂性，仅依赖样品统计数据难以充分支撑关键变量作用解释与结果追溯，而相关工艺条件、形貌特征和生长机理信息又长期分散于文献、实验记录及图像表征结果之中，难以形成有效的知识支撑。受此影响，碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌关系研究仍缺少稳定且可追溯的分析基础。

面向碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌关系分析，本文引入知识增强方法，将多源知识组织、自动化形貌表征与关系分析解释贯通起来。针对学术文献、实验记录、工艺参数表、结构化实验数据及图像表征结果长期分散且难以直接调用的问题，本文建立统一规范化处理与关系约束检索方法，构建最小语义事实单元及其关联结构，实现工艺分析、形貌解释和预测解释中的证据检索与结果追溯，为后续工艺变量收敛和机理分析提供知识基础。进一步围绕 SEM 图像中复杂细长结构前景提取困难的问题，构建兼顾连通性保持的前景分割方法，并结合骨架分析与几何计算建立样品级量化流程，提取密度、取向度、表观直径、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度等形貌特征，通过与人工分级结果的一致性比较验证样品级形貌标签的有效性。在此基础上，本文在当前基于 \ce{Al2O3}/Fe 催化层的 CVD 样品体系内，围绕 Fe 功率、Fe 厚度和退火时间开展统计分析与随机森林建模，考察三项工艺变量与取向度、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度等样品级形貌指标之间的可学习对应关系，并结合知识库中的机理证据解释关键变量作用。

实验结果表明，在当前基于 \ce{Al2O3}/Fe 催化层的 CVD 样品体系中，退火时间与取向度下降及弯曲相关特征增强之间呈现出更稳定的趋势对应关系，而 Fe 功率和 Fe 厚度更多表现为对不同工艺区间响应的联合调节。基于此，本文进一步设计并实现科研辅助分析平台，集成多源数据管理、知识检索增强、图像处理和预测分析等模块，完成方法集成与流程验证，形成了面向碳纳米管阵列工艺—形貌分析的可追溯研究链路。

\end{abstract}

\begin{enabstract}

In recent years, the development of artificial intelligence, especially large language models, has provided new technical means for knowledge acquisition, pattern discovery, and assisted analysis in materials science. Carbon nanotube (CNT) arrays have broad application prospects in energy storage devices, thermal interface materials, field emission devices, and high-performance composites because of their excellent electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties. However, the preparation of CNT arrays is jointly affected by multiple process parameters, and the evolution of morphology is complex. Meanwhile, the analysis of scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images still relies heavily on manual experience, and it remains difficult to stably quantify morphology features and accurately characterize process-morphology relationships. For this type of scientific task, general large language models are further limited by insufficient domain knowledge coverage, weak factual constraints, and poor traceability, making it difficult to directly satisfy the requirements of scientific analysis for accuracy, evidence support, and result traceability. Therefore, this thesis takes CNT arrays as the research object, regards process-morphology relationship analysis as the core task, and focuses on multi-source knowledge organization, sample-level morphology characterization, and knowledge-enhanced relationship analysis and interpretation.

To address the problem that multi-source information in CNT array research is scattered, heterogeneous, and difficult to directly serve scientific analysis, this thesis proposes a multi-source knowledge organization and retrieval-augmented method. Academic literature, experimental records, process parameter tables, structured experimental data, and image analysis results are first normalized in a unified manner. A Minimum Semantic Fact Unit representation and its association structure are then constructed. On this basis, a relation-constrained retrieval method is designed to support evidence retrieval and result traceability in process analysis, morphology interpretation, and prediction explanation, thereby providing a knowledge foundation for process-variable screening and mechanism analysis.

To address the problem that SEM images of CNT arrays are structurally complex and that manual interpretation is costly and inconsistent, this thesis proposes an automated morphology characterization method. An improved foreground segmentation method is developed for complex slender structures. Combined with skeleton analysis and geometric computation, a sample-level quantification pipeline is established to extract morphology features including density, alignment, apparent diameter, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity. The effectiveness of the method is further validated through consistency comparison with manual grading results, thus providing stable inputs for subsequent process-morphology relationship analysis.

To address the problem that relationships between process parameters and morphology features are complex and that analytical results lack explanatory support, this thesis establishes a knowledge-enhanced method for process-morphology relationship analysis and prediction. With retrieval support, the analysis is focused on Fe power, Fe thickness, and annealing time. Statistical relationship analysis and multi-feature modeling are conducted for these variables within the current CVD sample system based on \ce{Al2O3}/Fe catalyst layers. The study examines whether these three variables can form learnable correspondences with sample-level morphology indicators, including alignment, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity. Mechanistic evidence from the knowledge base is then used to interpret the effects of key variables and to provide traceable support for the analytical conclusions. Results on the current sample system show that annealing time exhibits a more stable trend correspondence with decreasing alignment and increasing bending-related morphology features, while Fe power and Fe thickness mainly act as joint adjustment variables across different process regions.

On this basis, a scientific research assistant analysis platform is designed and implemented to integrate multi-source data management, knowledge retrieval augmentation, image processing, and predictive analysis. Experimental results show that the proposed methods can achieve multi-source knowledge organization, automated quantification of morphology features, and process-parameter-driven process-morphology relationship analysis and prediction, thereby providing support for process control, morphology characterization, and mechanism analysis of CNT arrays.

\end{enabstract}
