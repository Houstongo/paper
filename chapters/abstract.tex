\begin{abstract}

近年来，人工智能特别是大模型技术的发展，为材料科学中的知识获取、规律发现与辅助分析提供了新的技术手段。碳纳米管阵列因具备优良的电学、热学和力学性能，在储能器件、热界面材料、场发射器件及高性能复合材料等领域具有广阔的应用前景。然而，碳纳米管阵列的制备过程受多种工艺参数共同影响，形貌演化规律复杂；同时，扫描电子显微镜图像分析仍在较大程度上依赖人工经验，关键形貌的稳定量化以及工艺参数与形貌之间关系的准确刻画仍面临困难。对于这类科研场景，通用大模型又容易受到领域知识覆盖不足和事实约束能力有限等因素影响，难以直接满足科研任务对准确性、证据支撑和结果追溯的要求。基于此，本文以碳纳米管阵列为研究对象，以工艺—形貌关系分析为核心任务，以知识增强为支撑手段，围绕多源知识组织、样品级关键形貌提取、工艺—形貌关系分析与结果解释展开研究。

针对碳纳米管阵列研究中多源信息分散、异构性强且难以直接支撑科研分析的问题，本文提出面向 CNT 阵列研究的多源知识建模与检索增强方法。面向学术文献、实验记录、工艺参数表、结构化实验数据及图像分析结果，构建统一的知识规范化处理流程；提出基于层次化切分的最小语义事实单元表示方法，建立工艺参数、关键形貌、生长机理与文献证据之间的关联结构，并设计面向科研任务的关系约束检索算法，实现工艺分析、形貌解释和预测解释等任务中的证据链检索与结果追溯，为后续工艺变量筛选和机理分析提供知识支撑。

针对碳纳米管阵列扫描电子显微镜图像形貌复杂、人工判读成本较高且一致性不足的问题，本文提出自动化关键形貌表征方法。基于前景分割结果，结合骨架分析与几何计算，构建样品级量化流程，提取密度、取向度、表观直径、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度等关键特征，将依赖经验判断的形貌信息转化为可比较的结构化表征，并通过与人工分级结果的一致性比较验证方法的有效性，为后续工艺—形貌关系分析提供稳定的标签基础。

针对碳纳米管阵列制备过程中工艺参数与关键形貌之间关系复杂、结果解释支撑不足的问题，本文建立知识增强支持下的工艺—形貌关系分析与预测方法。在知识检索结果支持下，收敛工艺变量分析范围，重点围绕 Fe 功率、Fe 厚度和退火时长开展统计关系分析与多特征建模；构建工艺参数与取向度、平均曲率、波曲度和迂曲度之间的非线性映射，实现关键形貌指标预测；结合知识库中的机理证据，对关键变量作用及相关分析结果进行解释，为结论提供可追溯的证据支撑。

在此基础上，本文进一步设计并实现科研辅助分析平台，集成多源数据管理、知识检索增强、图像处理和预测分析等模块。实验结果表明，本文方法能够实现多源知识统一组织、关键形貌自动化量化以及工艺参数驱动的工艺—形貌关系分析与预测，为分析工艺参数变化与阵列形貌演化之间的关系提供支持。

\end{abstract}

\begin{enabstract}

In recent years, the development of artificial intelligence, especially large models, has provided new technical means for knowledge acquisition, pattern discovery, and assisted analysis in materials science. Carbon nanotube (CNT) arrays have broad application prospects in energy storage devices, thermal interface materials, field emission devices, and high-performance composites because of their excellent electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties. However, the preparation of CNT arrays is jointly affected by multiple process parameters, and the evolution of morphology is complex. At the same time, the analysis of scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images still relies heavily on manual experience, and it remains difficult to stably quantify key morphologies and accurately characterize the relationships between process parameters and morphology. For this type of scientific task, general large models are also limited by insufficient domain knowledge coverage and weak factual constraints, making it difficult to directly satisfy the requirements of scientific research for accuracy, evidence support, and result traceability. Therefore, this thesis takes CNT arrays as the research object, regards process-morphology relationship analysis as the core task, uses knowledge enhancement as the supporting approach, and focuses on multi-source knowledge organization, sample-level key morphology extraction, process-morphology relationship analysis, and result interpretation.

To address the problem that multi-source information in CNT array research is scattered, heterogeneous, and difficult to directly support scientific analysis, this thesis proposes a multi-source knowledge modeling and retrieval-augmented method for CNT array research. A unified knowledge normalization process is constructed for academic literature, experimental records, process parameter tables, structured experimental data, and image analysis results. A Minimum Semantic Fact Unit representation based on hierarchical segmentation is proposed to establish the association structure among process parameters, key morphologies, growth mechanisms, and literature evidence. A relation-constrained retrieval algorithm is further designed for scientific tasks, enabling evidence-chain retrieval and result traceability in process analysis, morphology interpretation, and prediction explanation, and providing knowledge support for subsequent process-variable screening and mechanism analysis.

To address the problem that SEM images of CNT arrays have complex morphologies, while manual interpretation is costly and inconsistent, this thesis proposes an automated key morphology characterization method. Based on foreground segmentation results and combined with skeleton analysis and geometric computation, a sample-level quantification pipeline is constructed to extract key features including density, alignment, apparent diameter, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity. The method transforms morphology information that previously depended on empirical judgment into comparable structured representations, and its effectiveness is verified through consistency comparison with manual grading results, thus providing stable labels for subsequent process-morphology relationship analysis.

To address the problem that the relationships between process parameters and key morphologies are complex and that analytical results lack explanatory support, this thesis establishes a knowledge-enhanced method for process-morphology relationship analysis and prediction. With the support of retrieval results, the analysis is focused on Fe power, Fe thickness, and annealing time. Statistical relationship analysis and multi-feature modeling are carried out for these variables. Nonlinear mappings between process parameters and morphology indicators, including alignment, average curvature, waviness, and tortuosity, are constructed to predict key morphology indicators. Mechanistic evidence from the knowledge base is then used to interpret the effects of key variables and related analytical results, providing traceable evidence for the conclusions.

On this basis, a scientific research assistant analysis platform is further designed and implemented to integrate multi-source data management, knowledge retrieval augmentation, image processing, and predictive analysis. Experimental results show that the proposed methods can achieve unified organization of multi-source knowledge, automated quantification of key morphologies, and process-parameter-driven process-morphology relationship analysis and prediction, thereby supporting the analysis of the relationship between process-parameter variation and morphology evolution in CNT arrays.

\end{enabstract}
