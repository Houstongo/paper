\begin{abstract}

近年来，人工智能尤其是大模型技术的发展，为材料科学研究中的知识获取、规律挖掘和辅助分析带来了新的方法。碳纳米管阵列因其优异的电学、热学和力学性能，在储能器件、热界面材料、场发射器件及高性能复合材料等领域具有重要应用价值。然而，在碳纳米管阵列这类专业性强、机理复杂的科研场景中，通用大模型受领域知识覆盖不足和事实约束缺失影响，易出现知识幻觉、证据不足和分析结果不稳定等问题，难以满足科研任务对准确性、可追溯性和可解释性的要求。同时，碳纳米管阵列制备过程受温度、气体流量、催化剂状态和生长时间等多种工艺参数耦合作用影响，形貌演化规律复杂；扫描电子显微镜图像分析较多依赖人工经验，工艺参数与形貌特征之间的映射关系也较难准确刻画。针对上述问题，本文围绕碳纳米管阵列研究中的知识增强分析、形貌特征量化与工艺参数驱动预测展开，主要内容如下：

（1）针对碳纳米管领域知识来源分散、异构性强、难以直接支撑科研分析的问题，提出面向碳纳米管阵列研究的多源知识建模与检索增强方法。面向学术文献、实验记录、工艺参数表、结构化实验数据及图像分析结果，构建统一的知识规范化处理流程；设计基于边界优先级的层次化切分方法与元数据增强知识单元表示方法；建立工艺参数—形貌特征—生长机理—文献证据的知识关联结构；结合混合检索与重排序策略，为科研任务提供可追溯的知识检索结果。

（2）针对碳纳米管阵列扫描电子显微镜图像形貌复杂、人工评价主观性强的问题，研究自动化形貌特征提取方法。围绕平均曲率、取向度、直径分布和覆盖密度等核心指标，结合数字图像处理与图像分析技术，构建面向碳纳米管阵列的形貌特征量化流程，将形貌信息由定性描述转化为定量表征，为后续预测建模提供结构化数据。

（3）针对现有形貌预测方法机理支撑不足、结果解释能力较弱的问题，建立基于化学气相沉积工艺参数的碳纳米管阵列形貌预测模型。通过构建工艺参数与形貌特征之间的非线性映射关系，预测多项核心形貌指标；引入知识增强分析机制，利用知识库中检索得到的生长机理与文献证据对预测结果进行辅助解释与合理性分析，提高预测结果的可解释性。

（4）围绕碳纳米管阵列研究过程中的集成化应用需求，设计并实现智能分析平台。平台集成多源数据管理、知识检索增强、图像处理与预测分析等核心模块，形成从知识组织、形貌量化到预测分析的一体化研究流程。实验结果表明，本文方法能够完成多源知识统一组织、形貌特征自动化量化以及工艺参数驱动的形貌预测，并为分析工艺参数变化与阵列形貌演化之间的联系提供依据。

\end{abstract}

\begin{enabstract}

In recent years, the development of artificial intelligence, especially large model technology, has provided new technical means for knowledge acquisition, pattern mining, and auxiliary analysis in materials science research. Carbon nanotube (CNT) arrays, with their excellent electrical, thermal, and mechanical properties, have significant application value in fields such as energy storage devices, thermal interface materials, field emission devices, and high-performance composite materials. However, in highly specialized and mechanism-complex scientific research scenarios such as CNT arrays, general large models are prone to issues like knowledge hallucination, insufficient evidence, and unstable analysis results due to inadequate domain knowledge coverage and the absence of factual constraints. This makes it difficult to meet the requirements of accuracy, traceability, and interpretability in scientific research tasks. At the same time, the preparation process of CNT arrays is influenced by the coupling of various process parameters like temperature, gas flow rate, catalyst state, and growth time, resulting in complex morphology evolution. Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) image analysis relies heavily on manual experience, and the mapping relationship between process parameters and morphology features is difficult to characterize accurately. To address these problems, this thesis conducts research on knowledge-enhanced analysis, morphology feature quantization, and process parameter-driven prediction in CNT array research. The main contents are as follows:

(1) To address the issues of scattered and strongly heterogeneous knowledge sources in the CNT field that are difficult to directly support scientific analysis, a multi-source knowledge modeling and retrieval-augmented method for CNT array research is proposed. A unified knowledge normalization process is constructed for academic literature, experimental records, process parameter tables, structured experimental data, and image analysis results. A hierarchical segmentation method based on boundary priority and a metadata-enhanced knowledge unit representation method are designed. A knowledge association structure among process parameters, morphology features, growth mechanisms, and literature evidence is established. Combined with hybrid retrieval and reranking strategies, knowledge calling and analysis support for scientific research tasks are achieved.

(2) To address the issues of complex morphology in SEM images of CNT arrays and high subjectivity in manual evaluation, an automated morphology feature extraction method is studied. Centering on core indicators such as average curvature, degree of alignment, diameter distribution, and coverage density, a morphology feature quantization process for CNT arrays is constructed by combining digital image processing and image analysis techniques, transforming morphology information from qualitative description to quantitative characterization and providing a structured data foundation for subsequent predictive modeling.

(3) To address the issues of insufficient mechanism support and weak interpretability in existing morphology prediction methods, a morphology prediction model for CNT arrays based on Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) process parameters is established. By constructing nonlinear mapping relationships between process parameters and morphology features, the prediction of multiple core morphology indicators is achieved. A knowledge-enhanced analysis mechanism is introduced, utilizing growth mechanisms and literature evidence retrieved from the knowledge base to provide auxiliary explanation and rationality analysis for prediction results, enhancing the interpretability and scientific support capability of the predictions.

(4) Centering on the integrated application requirements in the research process of CNT arrays, an intelligent analysis platform is designed and implemented. Core modules such as multi-source data management, knowledge retrieval augmentation, image processing, and predictive analysis are integrated, forming an integrated research workflow from knowledge organization and morphology quantization to prediction support. Experimental results demonstrate that the method proposed in this thesis achieves unified organization of multi-source knowledge, automated quantization of morphology features, and process parameter-driven morphology prediction, contributing to revealing the intrinsic links between process parameter variations and array morphology evolution.

\end{enabstract}
